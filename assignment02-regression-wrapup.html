Question 1: Describe the data

What information is contained in this data set? Describe at least four variables (excluding year), including what they represent and their data type. Lastly, describe whether you think year would be a useful predictor in this data.

Answer:
This data set includes information detailing various size measurements, sex, and home islands for Adelie, Chinstrap, and Gentoo penguins. For example, the penguins’ measurements are noted through the following variables:

  • bill_length_mm: length of the penguin’s bill measured in millimeters (mm), datatype = float

  • bill_depth_mm: depth of the penguin’s bill measured in millimeters (mm), datatype = float

  • flipper_length_mm: length of the penguin’s flipper measured in millimeters (mm), datatype = integer

  • body_mass_g: the weight of the penguin measured in grams (g), datatype = integer

I believe year would be a useful predictor in the data because year can help detect long-term changes in any possible evolutionary patterns found in the penguins. Additionally, it can help find patterns in trends over time such as climate change.

For example, for every 1 gram increase in body mass, the log odds of being male increase by 0.00296 and every 1 millimeter increase in bill length, the log odds of being male increase by 0.14589. This means that penguins that are heavier and have longer bills are more likely to be male, whereas penguins with longer flippers are more likely to be female.
